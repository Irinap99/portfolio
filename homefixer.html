Home Fixer

Crafted as part of my UI/UX practice in Figma, this project explores how people can book reliable handymen through a modern, intuitive app. The focus was on reducing stress around finding trustworthy professionals and making the booking flow as easy as ordering a ride.

See full case study

Design Process: I mapped the user journey and flow to identify friction points, then iterated through wireframes and high-fidelity mockups. My design choices emphasized clarity, minimal navigation, and a consistent structure to make booking feel effortless.

Challenges: One challenge was making the flow transparent yet lightweight — users need trust signals (ratings, reviews, verification), but too much information risks overwhelming them. I solved this by prioritizing essential details at decision points (during provider comparison). Another challenge was designing for both sides of the marketplace (users and handymen), which required balancing simplicity with professional career features.

Technologies Used: Figma for wireframing, prototyping, and building the design system. Photoshop for mockups, image editing.

User Goals & Flows Home Fixer was designed to make booking handymen simple, reliable, and stress-free. Users aim to quickly find and compare trusted professionals, book services, and manage ongoing appointments with ease. Handymen seek visibility, credibility, and a streamlined way to showcase their skills. The main flow: browse services → compare providers → book → manage bookings → leave a review—ensures the experience is intuitive, efficient, and trustworthy at every step.

Features: Clean, minimal booking flow with clear service categories, built-in trust signals (reviews, verification, ratings), messaging and calling features for extra details between users and providers, career pages for handymen and corporate jobs to onboard and gain visibility, blog for home tips to extend value beyond just repairs

Future Improvements: In future iterations, the app could include personalized recommendations based on user history, in-app payment integration for a seamless booking experience. Additional features for handymen, such as advanced scheduling tools and analytics on completed jobs, could further enhance usability and value for both sides of the platform.
